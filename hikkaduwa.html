Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery


The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a popular conservation center located in Hikkaduwa, a coastal town in Sri Lanka. The hatchery plays a vital role in protecting sea turtles and their eggs, as well as raising awareness about the importance of marine conservation.At the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, visitors can learn about various species of sea turtles, including the Olive Ridley, Loggerhead, Green, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles. The hatchery focuses on rescuing turtle eggs that are vulnerable to predation and relocates them to protected areas until they hatch. This practice helps increase the survival rate of the hatchlings and contributes to the preservation of these endangered species.Visitors can witness the different stages of turtle development, from observing the eggs in the incubation tanks to seeing the adorable baby turtles as they make their way into the ocean for the first time. The hatchery staff provides educational tours, sharing valuable information about the life cycle, behavior, and threats faced by sea turtles.In addition to learning about sea turtles, the hatchery offers the opportunity for visitors to release baby turtles into the ocean.

This hands-on experience allows individuals to actively participate in conservation efforts and directly contribute to the survival of these fascinating creatures.Furthermore, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery often collaborates with local communities and fishermen to promote sustainable fishing practices and reduce the harm caused to sea turtles by fishing nets and other human activities. They also run programs to raise awareness about marine pollution and the importance of maintaining a clean and healthy ocean environment.Overall, a visit to the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery offers a unique and memorable experience, allowing visitors to appreciate the beauty of sea turtles while supporting their conservation and protection.
